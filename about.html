The Long Version

2020 – 2024 Phoenix Zoo

Where the work started

A zoo is in the experience business. People come to escape, make memories, and hopefully walk out actually caring about the natural world — which is the real mission. At a nonprofit, that care has a direct feedback loop: a great day becomes a return visit, which becomes gate revenue, which funds animal care, enclosure upgrades, and the next experience worth building. You can’t please everyone, but you can please most. The measure that mattered most wasn’t what someone said at the exit gate — it was how the visit made them feel long after they’d left, and whether they came back.

Over four years managing 100+ staff and 1.4 million guests a year, I was also running a weekly newsletter to reach part-timers spread across shifts from 8am to 11pm, redesigning training documents so new staff could learn a station fast and find answers faster once out in the field, building graphics for guests with questions in the moment, and putting together slide decks for the CEO and board every time we wanted to change something. Nobody called it UX. It was all UX.

2023 – Present ASU · Human Systems Engineering

Putting a name to it

The turning point wasn’t a single moment — it was recognizing a pattern in work I’d already been doing. The weekly newsletter was communication design for a fragmented audience. The training documents were information architecture. The guest-facing graphics were visual design for real users in real contexts. The board decks were stakeholder communication. Once I could name what I’d been doing, the next step was obvious.

I switched from a conservation biology and ecology degree to Human Systems Engineering — partly because the zoo work had made it clear where my skills actually lived, and partly because I have a kid to take care of and wanted a career that could hold that. Best of both worlds: still working in service of what I cared about at the zoo, with a set of tools that can actually get me there.

Now Precision Air & Plumbing

Same instincts, different building

These days I’m the first voice a customer hears and one of the last sets of eyes on an invoice before it goes out. Different industry, same instinct: spot where the process breaks down for the person on the other end of it, and fix it before it becomes a pattern.
